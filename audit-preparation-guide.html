Audit Preparation Guide

Overview

Regulatory audits ensure that CAMOs comply with EASA Part-M and Part-CAMO requirements. Proper preparation helps avoid findings and demonstrates robust airworthiness control.

Audit Types

  • Internal Audits: Conducted by QA for internal compliance
  • Authority Audits: Performed by EASA or national authorities
  • Customer Audits: Lessors, airlines, or MRO partners

Pre-Audit Preparation

  • Notify departments and assign focal points
  • Review previous findings and corrective actions
  • Ensure access to current AMP, AD/SB records, and MEL
  • Verify all forms, reports, and logs are complete and signed

During the Audit

  • Provide requested records promptly
  • Answer questions clearly and honestly
  • Take notes on observations and minor findings
  • Clarify misunderstandings on the spot if possible

Post-Audit Actions

  • Log findings with classification (Level 1 / Level 2)
  • Define root cause and corrective/preventive action
  • Assign owners and due dates
  • Submit closure evidence to the auditing body
