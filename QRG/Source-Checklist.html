CAMO Admin Source Checklist

This checklist helps CAMO Technical Administrators ensure airworthiness tasks are updated based on their origin source. Use this Quick Reference to maintain compliance with EASA Part-M and Part-CAMO.

1. Airworthiness Directives (ADs)

  • ☐ Checked for new ADs from EASA or FAA
  • ☐ Entered AD into Maintenance Tracking System
  • ☐ Verified AD applicability and compliance deadline
  • ☐ Linked documentation and status to aircraft

2. Service Bulletins (SBs)

  • ☐ Verified SBs from OEM or lessor
  • ☐ Assessed applicability and modification status
  • ☐ Entered SB into the tracking system
  • ☐ Documented status (Not Applicable / Open / Complied)

3. AMP Tasks (Scheduled Maintenance)

  • ☐ Confirmed AMP is updated with latest revision
  • ☐ Logged tasks due by FH, FC, or calendar limits
  • ☐ Updated task completion status after maintenance

4. Defect Log / MEL / CDL

  • ☐ Captured all tech log entries and pilot reports
  • ☐ Categorized MEL/CDL correctly and tracked deferment
  • ☐ Updated defect status (Open / Deferred / Closed)

5. Component / LLP Monitoring

  • ☐ Updated hours/cycles after each flight
  • ☐ Logged any component changes (removed/installed)
  • ☐ Verified LLP tracking and archive traceability

6. Audit & Compliance Outputs

  • ☐ CRS collected and linked to completed tasks
  • ☐ Work orders properly filed and referenced
  • ☐ Documents prepared for ARC renewal or audits
